Located in Reykjavík Iceland, Paladin was formed back in 2017. The group draws its influence from fantasy literature and the heavy metal greats of the 80s and 90s.

The band has made a good name in the Icelandic metal scene with their live performances and demo releases, which landed them a spot at both of Iceland’s main underground festivals; Eistnaflug and Norðanpaunk and subsequently one of Iceland’s biggest music festivals, Iceland Airwaves in 2019. Magný Rós Sigurðardóttir, the general manager of Eistnaflug pointed out the unity the band seems to create during it’s concerts in an interview with the Reykjavík Grapevine:

“...Another standout are the power metal breakout stars of Paladin. ‘That’s a new band, but looking at the crowd during the first ever Paladin concert, they were having so much fun,’ Magný reminisces. ‘You had all kinds of metalheads singing along with their fists in the air—black metal, viking metal, death metal people—I’m looking forward to that.’”

(Lucky 15: Eistnaflug 2019 Goes Back To Basics - The Reykjavík Grapevine)

Paladin has released two demos out on youtube so far but is now in the process of recording their first full length album. Around 8 songs are expected to be included on the album showcasing the band's variety in songwriting with titles such as: Ride the distant storm, Evermore and The Dark Crystal.

The album is expected to be released in the coming year.
